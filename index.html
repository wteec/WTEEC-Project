WTEEC Project

Wireless Energy Transfer for EV Charging (WTEEC)

Revolutionizing EV Charging

About our project

  • Current EV charging is limited to stationary vehicles
  • Dynamic wireless power transfer (DWPT) still in its infancy
  • Need for a solution to charge electric vehicles while in motion
  • There are technical challenges like energy loss, efficiency in varied conditions, and high costs
  • We aim to improve EV viability for long-distnace travel
  • DWPT system uses adaptive control for real-time adjustments
  • Resonant coils embedded in roads and installed on vehicles

Our goals

  • Develop an adaptive control algorithm for real-time adjestments in power transfer
  • Enhance power transfer efficiency in various driving conditions
  • Incorporate safety measures like thermal management and system feedback loops
  • Minimize energy loss and improve misalignment tolerance

Our approach

  • Real-time adjustments based on vehicle speed, coil misalignment, and distance
  • Use of high-frequency AC power transfer through resonant coils
  • Transmitter and receiver coils, power electronics, AC to DC conversion
  • Thermal management, cooling systems, and feedback loops for optimal performance

Testing

  • Computer simulations to validate the effectiveness of the adaptive algorithms
  • Simulate various scenarios like different vehicle speeds and misalignment
  • Build small-scale test track with transmitter coils
  • Test vehicle model equipped with receiver coils for power transfer efficiency
  • Measure energy transfer, system response, and heat generation in real-world conditions
  • Stress-test under varied traffic, weather, and misalignment conditions
